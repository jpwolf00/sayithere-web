☕ At a café in Paris 🇫🇷 French

Order a coffee and a croissant

Bonjour, un café et un croissant, s'il vous plaît.

bohn-ZHOOR, uhn kah-FAY ay uhn krwah-SAHN, seel voo PLEH

Cultural note In France, always say "Bonjour" when you enter a café or shop — it's considered rude not to greet. "Un café" means a small espresso; ask for "un café allongé" if you want a longer coffee.

You might hear back

Un café et un croissant, très bien. uhn kah-FAY ay uhn krwah-SAHN, treh byahn A coffee and a croissant, very well.

Café de Flore

Paris, France

"Bonjour, un chocolat chaud, s'il vous plaît."

Café de Flore is famous for its thick, rich hot chocolate, made with real melted chocolate — not cocoa powder. It arrives with a small jug of extra hot chocolate on the side.

Senso-ji Temple

Tokyo, Japan

"おまもりは どこで かえますか？"

Where do I buy omamori charms? — おまもり is the general term for protective charms and amulets sold at temples and shrines.

Grand Bazaar

Istanbul, Turkey

"Halı dükkanında pazarlık bekleniyor mu?"

Is haggling expected at carpet shops? — Haggling is part of the experience at the Grand Bazaar. Start at about half the asking price.

Prague, Czech Republic

"Je ten stůl u okna s výhledem na zrcadlo volný?"

Is the window table with mirror view free? — Tables by the large mirror are popular, but staff will indicate if one is available.
